I'm looking for ...

... an old story or obituary that was in The Register-Guard a while back.

Search for it here, in Google's News Archive (Search Tip: On the Google search page, be sure to adjust your beginning and end search dates as the default setting is only for 30 days back.) The archive is The Register-Guard microfilm collection available online, going back to 1867. If you know the approximate date the item appeared, you can narrow your search to a range of dates or you can browse newspapers by date. Note: As of May 2011, Google has shut down the news archive project as well as adding new content, but the existing archive should remain available.

Please note that currently the Google News Archive has no printing or downloading capability, but you can e-mail your results. For printable and downloadable results, try our ProQuest archive, which goes back to November 2001. Searches are free but you must pay a small fee to retrieve the content.

Another useful tool is The Register-Guard online index maintained by The University of Oregon libraries. It covers the years 1963-2003. The results will only tell you the date and page on which an item appeared, but it may turn up something a Google search doesn't. Once you know the date of publication, you can use that date in either the ProQuest archive for a downloadable copy or the Google Archive Search for a browser-only image of the printed edition.

... a copy of the actual newspaper that contains an item that appeared in The Register-Guard years ago.

Unfortunately, newspaper copies are only kept for about 30 days. To obtain a facsimile of the page, if you're in the area, try the microfiche collections at either the Eugene Public or University of Oregon libraries. If you're out of the area, the Google News Archive is your best bet.

... an obituary that appeared within the last 30 days.

The 30-day obituary index is here.

... a story that appeared in The Register-Guard within the last seven days.

Look for it here, the Register-Guard seven-day archive of staff-written stories. If you're looking for a national, international or Washington, D.C.-based wire-service news item or an op-ed column by a non-local columnist, a Google search is probably your best bet.
